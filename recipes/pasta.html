Pasta

Pasta is a versatile and beloved Italian dish made from various types of dough, often based on wheat, water, and sometimes eggs. It comes in a wide range of shapes and sizes, each with its own unique texture and ability to hold different sauces. Pasta is typically cooked in boiling water until it reaches the desired tenderness, and it's commonly served with an assortment of sauces, including marinara, Alfredo, pesto, and more. It's a staple of Italian cuisine and enjoyed worldwide for its comfort and variety.

Ingredients

  • Pasta of your choice
  • Sauce of your choice (marinara, Alfredo, pesto, etc.)
  • Optional toppings and additions (meat, vegetables, cheese)
  1. Bring a pot of water to a boil and add salt.
  2. Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook according to the package instructions until al dente.
  3. While the pasta cooks, prepare your chosen sauce and any toppings.
  4. Once the pasta is cooked, drain it and return it to the pot.
  5. Add the sauce and toppings to the pot and toss everything together to combine.
  6. Heat the pasta and sauce mixture for a short time to ensure everything is warmed through.
  7. Serve the pasta in bowls, optionally garnished with additional toppings and grated cheese.
